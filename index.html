3.1 The Role of Information Technology in Business

What is Information Technology?

Information Technology (IT) refers to the use of computers, software, mobile devices, networks and the Internet to collect, process, store and share information for business purposes.

Today, almost every business uses IT to improve efficiency and serve customers better.

Everyday examples: Starbucks (mobile ordering) · Grab (GPS & apps) · Shopee (online marketplace) · Maybank (MAE App) · ZUS Coffee (mobile ordering & loyalty).

The 6 Roles of IT in Business

The six roles of IT — straight from your chapter mind map
  • Communication — Email, WhatsApp Business, Microsoft Teams, Zoom for fast, effective contact.
  • Marketing — Facebook, TikTok, Instagram, websites & online ads to promote products widely.
  • Customer Service — Chatbots, live chat and FAQ give quick responses to customers.
  • Finance — Accounting software prepares invoices, records sales and generates reports.
  • Inventory — Systems monitor stock, reorder products and track deliveries.
  • Decision Making — IT provides accurate data and reports for better, faster decisions.
IT improves: efficiency · productivity · accuracy · customer satisfaction · competitive advantage.
  • IT improves communication
  • IT increases efficiency
  • IT reduces operating costs
  • IT improves customer service

3.2 E-Business and E-Commerce

E-Business

Using the internet and digital technology to manage ALL business activities.

Examples: online communication · online banking · supply chain management · human resource (HR) systems · customer relationship management (CRM).

E-Commerce

The buying and selling of products or services online.

Examples: Shopee · Lazada · TikTok Shop · Foodpanda · GrabFood.

3.3 Managing Information

What is Information?

Information is processed data that is meaningful and useful for making business decisions.

Raw data: "100 customers visited today."  →  Information: "Sales increased by 20%."
Good information, the information process, and its sources

Good Information Should Be

  • Accurate
  • Relevant
  • Complete
  • Reliable
  • Timely

Information Process

Collect→Store→ Process→Analyse→Make Decisions

Sources of Information

Internal: sales reports, inventory, financial records, employee data.
External: customer feedback, market trends, competitor information, government reports.

  • Good information leads to better decisions.
  • Poor information leads to poor decisions.

3.4 The Influence of Technology in Business

Two sides of the same coin — opportunity vs risk

✅ Positive Influence

  • Faster communication
  • Higher productivity
  • Better customer experience
  • Wider market reach
  • Encourages innovation & global business

❌ Negative Influence

  • Cybersecurity threats
  • Privacy concerns
  • Technology dependence
  • Job displacement
  • High initial cost & digital divide
Emerging technology to watch: AI · Cloud Computing · IoT (Internet of Things) · Big Data.
  • Technology creates opportunities.
  • Businesses must also manage the risks.

📚 Glossary — tap to expand

Information Technology (IT)
Use of computers, software, networks and the Internet to collect, process, store and share information for business.
E-Business
Using digital technology to manage all business activities — internal and external.
E-Commerce
The buying and selling of products or services online; a part of e-business.
B2B / B2C / C2C / C2B
The four directions of online trade: Business-to-Business, Business-to-Consumer, Consumer-to-Consumer, and Consumer-to-Business.
CRM
Customer Relationship Management — systems that manage a company's interactions with customers.
POS
Point of Sale — system used to record sales transactions at checkout.
Information
Processed data that is meaningful and useful for making business decisions.
Cloud Computing
Storing and accessing data and programs over the Internet instead of a local computer.
IoT (Internet of Things)
Everyday devices connected to the Internet that collect and exchange data.
Big Data
Very large data sets analysed to reveal patterns, trends and insights for decision making.
Cybersecurity
Protecting systems, networks and data from digital attacks and unauthorised access.
